You are working on Qleno, a multi-tenant SaaS platform for 
residential and commercial cleaning businesses operating across 
multiple US states and jurisdictions.

You are building the Employee Policy Configuration system — the 
settings layer that allows each tenant to configure their own 
pay structure, attendance rules, leave policies, and quality 
enforcement rules to match their specific company policies and 
local legal requirements.

CRITICAL ARCHITECTURE PRINCIPLE:
Qleno is a policy ENFORCEMENT engine, not a legal compliance engine.
The system never tells a tenant what law applies to them.
The system never auto-configures settings based on state or city.
All policy fields are blank or set to safe defaults on tenant creation.
The tenant's owner or HR administrator fills in every value.
Every configuration page must include a persistent disclaimer:
"These settings reflect your company policy. 
 Consult legal counsel to ensure compliance with applicable 
 federal, state, and local employment law."

TECH STACK: React 18, Tailwind, Express/Node.js, Supabase 
(Postgres + RLS), Plus Jakarta Sans (ONLY font).

DESIGN SYSTEM — NON-NEGOTIABLE:
- Background: #F7F6F3
- Cards: #FFFFFF
- Primary text: #1A1917
- Secondary text: #6B6860
- Borders: #E5E2DC
- Accent: var(--brand)
- NO emojis, NO dark mode, light theme only
- Plus Jakarta Sans only
- Status badges: pill shape, muted background, colored text
- Skeleton loaders for all async loads
- All monetary values: USD, 2 decimal places, comma-formatted

All endpoints: JWT protected, company_id scoped, RLS enforced.
