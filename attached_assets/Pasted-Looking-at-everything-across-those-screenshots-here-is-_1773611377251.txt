## What MC Has on the Client Profile That We Have Not Built Yet

**Quotes tab** — this is the online quoting system. A lead comes in, you build a quote, send it, they approve it, it converts to a job automatically. The quote has scope of work, pricing, frequency, and an agreement attached. This is how new clients enter the system without a phone call.

**Payments tab** — separate from billing history. This is where you see individual payment transactions, charge a card manually, issue refunds, and see payment method status. The billing tab shows invoices. The payments tab shows money movement.

**QuickBooks tab per client** — shows QBO connection status for that specific client, their QBO customer ID, outstanding balance pulled live from QBO, and overdue balance. This is per-client not just a global sync.

**Attachments tab** — file storage per client. Photos of the home, signed documents, inspection reports, anything the office needs to keep on file for that client.

**Property Management Groups** — you saw this on the User Account tab. This lets you group clients under a property management company. If one property manager sends you 12 units you can manage them all under one parent account with one billing contact.

**The "View as Customer" / portal preview** — you saw this in the client portal screenshots. The admin can click one button and see exactly what the client sees in their portal. No logging out and back in.

Three things worth flagging before you run this.

The Stripe subscription flow is the most important one financially. Right now you are building a product you cannot charge for. That needs to exist before you show this to your first outside tenant.

The batch invoicing combined with auto-charge is what saves your office staff an hour every single day. MC users say it is one of the top three things they love about the software. When Maribel finishes her day she clicks one button, all that day's jobs get invoiced, all cards on file get charged, all receipts go out. Zero manual work.

The eSign feature eliminates a real cost. DocuSign charges per envelope. For a company sending 50+ new client agreements per year that is meaningful money. Built-in eSign is legally equivalent under the ESIGN Act and costs you nothing per signature.
