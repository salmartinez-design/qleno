Smart decision. Keeping all client communication inside Qleno means you have a full audit trail, your techs never build personal relationships with clients outside the system, and you can see every message if there is ever a dispute.
Here is what this means architecturally:
What you want:
	∙	Techs and office can send SMS to clients from inside Qleno
	∙	The message comes from your PHES business number — not from anyone’s personal phone
	∙	All messages are logged in the client communication log
	∙	Nobody needs to share their personal number
What you need for this:
Your current MaidCentral SMS number — this is a Twilio number that MC provisioned for you. When you cancel MC, that number goes away unless you port it to your own Twilio account first.
Action required before April 5:
Contact MaidCentral support and ask them:
	1.	What is the phone number currently used for SMS notifications sent from our account
	2.	Can we port that number out to our own Twilio account before cancellation
If MC will not release the number — which is possible — you get a new Twilio number and send a one-time message to all clients from the old MC system before you cancel: “PHES Cleaning now sends messages from [new number]. Please save this number.”

Here is the addition to the job panel prompt:

ADDITION — SMS from job panel

Add an SMS button to Section 2 of the job panel 
next to the phone number.

Two buttons side by side:
- Phone icon button — taps to call (tel: link)
- SMS icon button — taps to open SMS compose 
  within Qleno (not the device native SMS app)

The SMS button opens a small compose sheet 
from the bottom of the screen with:
- "To: [Client Name] ([phone number])" — read only
- Message text area with placeholder: 
  "Type a message..."
- Quick message chips above the keyboard 
  for common messages:
  "On my way" 
  "Running 15 minutes late"
  "Outside your home"
  "Job complete — thank you"
  Tapping a chip fills the message area
- Send button

On send:
- POST /api/communications/sms with 
  customer_id, job_id, message, direction='outbound'
- Message is sent via Twilio from the 
  company's configured FROM number 
  (TWILIO_FROM_NUMBER in company settings)
- Message is logged to communicationsTable 
  with type='sms', direction='outbound', 
  sent_by=current user id, job_id
- Success confirmation: "Message sent"

The client receives the SMS from the PHES 
business number — never from a personal phone.

Inbound replies from clients also log to 
communicationsTable via Twilio webhook at 
POST /api/communications/sms/webhook.

The full SMS thread for each client is 
visible in the customer profile 
Communications tab.

IMPORTANT: Twilio is not yet configured.

On the number itself — do this today:
Log into MaidCentral, go to Company Settings or Notifications, and find the phone number listed as your SMS sender. Write it down. Then call Twilio directly at 1-888-589-9810 or go to twilio.com and create a free account. When you have the Twilio account, you can either port the MC number over or get a new local Chicago number for about $1/month.
Once you have the Twilio account you will have three values to add to Replit Secrets:
	∙	TWILIO_ACCOUNT_SID
	∙	TWILIO_AUTH_TOKEN
	∙	TWILIO_FROM_NUMBER
Add those and the SMS button activates immediately across the entire app — job panel, customer profile communications tab, and the notification triggers already built in Sprint 1.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
