**THE PROBLEM**

Nearly all text is rendering in `#6B6860` (secondary muted) regardless of its importance. Primary values, labels, counts, and sublabels are indistinguishable from each other. Fix by enforcing a strict 3-level hierarchy:

---

**HIERARCHY RULES — apply globally across the entire dashboard:**

**Level 1 — Primary values (the number you're supposed to read first)**
- Font-size: 22px for KPI tiles, 18px for secondary counts
- Font-weight: 500
- Color: `#1A1917`
- Applies to: revenue amounts, job counts, client counts, all chip numbers, all metric tile main values

**Level 2 — Labels and section headers**
- Font-size: 11px–12px
- Font-weight: 500
- Color: `#1A1917` at 70% — use `#4A4845` (not the full muted gray)
- Applies to: tile labels ("MONTHLY REVENUE", "AVG BILL", "NEXT 7 DAYS"), section headers ("TODAY'S STATUS", "KEY NUMBERS", "TECHS TODAY"), chip labels ("IN PROGRESS", "SCHEDULED"), column headers

**Level 3 — Supporting detail only**
- Font-size: 11px–12px
- Font-weight: 400
- Color: `#6B6860`
- Applies to: subtext only — "Last 30 days", "67 recurring", "X jobs on the books", date strings, "Clock Monitor →" links
