## SPRINT 9 — EMPLOYEE COMPENSATION AND PAYROLL

**9.1 — Residential compensation**
Pay = Residential Tech Pay % (default 35%) of job total ÷ number of techs assigned. Equal split always. Setting: "Residential Tech Pay %" in Payroll Options.

**9.2 — Commercial compensation**
Pay = Commercial Hourly Rate (default $20/hr) × hours.
Company default: Allowed Hours. Toggle per job: Allowed Hours or Worked Hours.
Settings: "Commercial Hourly Rate" = $20.00, "Commercial Pay Default" = Allowed Hours.

**9.3 — Training pay**
Training status flag on employee profile with start and end date (default hire date + 21 days). While active: flat $20/hr, no commission. Auto-expires on end date. Manually extendable by office or owner. Setting: "Training Pay Rate" = $20/hr.

**9.4 — 3-hour pay minimum**
Every dispatched job — including re-clean and recovery tech assignments — guarantees minimum 3 hours of pay at the applicable rate. At Close Day: if actual hours < 3, system pays 3 hours. If actual hours > 3, system pays actual hours. Setting: "Minimum Job Pay Hours" = 3.

**9.5 — Re-clean and recovery job types**
When valid quality complaint filed on completed job, office triggers Re-Clean workflow from job record. Three options:

**Option A — Original tech returns:**
- Original job pay reclassifies from commission to Re-Clean Tech Rate ($20/hr) × hours worked on original job
- Return visit = $0 additional pay. Returning to fix substandard work is a job obligation covered by the original job compensation. It is not a separately compensated event.
- Re-Clean job record created and linked to original job for tracking and dispatch purposes only — no pay record attached to original tech for the return visit
- Both records on dispatch board: original flagged Quality Issue, re-clean flagged Re-Clean type
- Original job payroll record updated: "Pay reclassified — quality issue, tech returned"

**Option B — Original tech refuses:**
- Original job pay reclassifies to $18/hr floor (company pay floor) × hours worked on original job
- Insubordination flag auto-added to employee profile with date, job record link, timestamp
- Office alert: "{{tech_name}} refused re-clean on job #{{job_id}}. Insubordination flag added to profile."
- Recovery tech assigned to new Re-Clean job record
- Recovery tech pay = Re-Clean Tech Rate ($20/hr) × whichever is greater: actual hours on site OR Minimum Job Pay Hours (default 3). Recovery tech on site 1 hour = paid 3 hours = $60.00 minimum.
- Recovery tech Re-Clean job on dispatch board as separate record with Re-Clean badge

**Option C — Original tech unavailable (not refusal — not disciplinary):**
- Original job pay reclassifies from commission to Re-Clean Tech Rate ($20/hr) × hours worked on original job
- No insubordination flag
- Recovery tech assigned, same pay rules as Option B
- Recovery tech Re-Clean job on dispatch board

**Settings in Payroll Options:**
- "Re-Clean Tech Rate" = $20/hr — floor rate, never a ceiling. If tech's normal rate is higher, pay higher.
- "Company Pay Floor" = $18/hr — used for insubordination reclassification in Option B
- "Unavailable Reclassification Rate" = $20/hr (same as Re-Clean Tech Rate, displayed separately for clarity)

All re-clean records labeled on dispatch board and payroll with Re-Clean badge and linked original job number.

**9.6 — Quality Probation**
Triggered when tech accumulates 2 quality complaints in rolling 30-day window.

When triggered:
- Quality Probation status flag on employee profile with start date
- Pay switches to Quality Probation Pay Rate ($20/hr flat) for all subsequent jobs — no commission
- Office notified: "{{tech_name}} entered Quality Probation as of {{date}}. Pay switched to $20/hr."
- Tech SMS: "Hi {{first_name}}, your account has been placed on Quality Probation. Please connect with the office team."

Exit: 30 consecutive days zero complaints → auto-exit. Pay reverts to commission-eligible. Office notified.
Probation history remains on employee profile permanently.
Settings: "Quality Probation Threshold" = 2/30 days, "Quality Probation Pay Rate" = $20/hr.

**9.7 — Skip/Lockout fee tech comp override**
Fee rule tech comp (flat $60 default) overrides residential % for that job only. If multiple techs assigned, split $60 equally.

**9.8 — Weekly minimum pay floor check**
The $18/hr floor is a weekly safety net — not a per-job calculation.

At the end of each pay week (Sunday through Saturday), when office runs payroll:
1. Sum all earnings for the tech that week: commission + tips + additional pay (all types combined)
2. Sum total hours worked that week across all jobs (clock-in to clock-out)
3. Calculate effective weekly rate: total earnings ÷ total hours worked
4. If effective rate ≥ $18/hr → pay as normal, no adjustment
5. If effective rate < $18/hr → calculate shortfall: ($18 × total hours) − total earnings = top-up amount
6. Add top-up as "Minimum Pay Adjustment" line item on payroll summary
7. Grand total for week is never below $18 × total hours worked

Example: Tech works 40 hours, earns $600 commission + $50 tips = $650 total. Effective rate = $650 ÷ 40 = $16.25/hr. Below $18 floor. Shortfall = ($18 × 40) − $650 = $720 − $650 = $70 top-up. Tech paid $720. Payroll shows: Commission $600 + Tips $50 + Minimum Pay Adjustment $70 = $720 grand total.

This check runs automatically before Close Week can be finalized. Office must acknowledge any top-up adjustments before closing the pay period.

Setting: "Company Pay Floor" = $18.00/hr, editable by owner only.

New section "Technician Compensation" in Payroll Options. All fields read from payroll_settings table at runtime. Owner only — office sees values but cannot edit:

- Residential Tech Pay %: [35]
- Commercial Hourly Rate: [$20.00]
- Commercial Pay Default: [Allowed Hours / Worked Hours toggle]
- Training Pay Rate: [$20.00]
- Minimum Job Pay Hours: [3]
- Re-Clean Tech Rate: [$20.00]
- Company Pay Floor: [$18.00] per hour — weekly safety net

**15.2 — Seat counting rules**
Active seat counts are calculated at billing cycle and in real time for display purposes.

Technician seats: count of employees with role = technician AND status = active for this company. Terminated, inactive, or suspended employees do not count. When an employee is marked inactive or terminated, their seat is freed immediately.

Office staff seats: count of users with role = office or role = admin for this company (excluding owner). Owner always has a free seat not counted against any limit.

These two pools are completely independent — technician count never affects office staff count and vice versa.

**15.3 — Seat enforcement rules**
When a new user is invited or an employee is activated:
- System checks current seat count against tier limit
- If within limit: allow, no charge until next billing cycle
- If at or above limit: show upgrade prompt OR allow with overage notification

When seat count exceeds included limit:
- Calculate overage: (current active count − included count) × overage rate
- Add overage to next month's Stripe invoice automatically
- Show current overage amount in Company Settings Billing tab in real time

Office staff and technician overages are calculated and billed independently.

**15.4 — Role enforcement — office staff cannot be technicians**
At the database and API level, enforce that a user with role = office or role = admin cannot:
- Be assigned to a job as a technician
- Appear on the dispatch board as a worker
- Clock in or out of a job
- Appear in any technician-facing mobile views
- Be counted in technician seat counts

This enforcement is permanent and cannot be bypassed by office staff. Only owner or Super Admin can change a user's role. If an office staff user attempts to access any technician function, return a 403 with message: "This action is restricted to technician accounts."
