Part 2

The whole day — three taps per house

One simple loop repeats at each home. There is no day clock to remember.

1
On My Way — when leaving for the house. Texts the client a live ETA and starts the drive leg (job A → job B).
2
Clock In — on arrival. Starts this job's time, with GPS to confirm they're on-site.
3
Clock Out — when the house is done. Stamps the actual job time. Then drive to the next house and repeat.
The day is derived, never a button. "Paid day" = first Clock In → last Clock Out. Checking out of the last house ends the day automatically — because there's no more activity, not because a button was pressed. A surprise add-on just extends it. This is also what guarantees the drive home is never paid.

Mileage — the rules, locked

  • Only the legs between jobs are paid. House A → House B counts. Home → first job and last job → home are commute and are never reimbursed.
  • Distance is the mapping API's driving distance between the two client addresses — not the cleaner's actual route. They cannot pad miles by driving around.
  • Dated rate table. A past week computes at the rate that was in effect then, never today's rate.
  • No money until reviewed. Computed mileage waits in a review gate; it only becomes pay when the office approves it. Every paid mile traces back to its leg.

Part 4

Idle time, & "what if they milk it?"

The one rule that protects you both ways: capturing ≠ paying.
Qleno records every minute — job, drive, idle — for visibility and the rare overtime check. But the only dollars that move are commission + mileage. Idle is a number you see, not a line you pay.

Idle = on-the-clock time that wasn't in a house and wasn't reimbursed driving (usually a lunch break). It is a record, not pay. Real meal breaks are excluded; on-duty waiting only matters in a 40+ hour week.

Why milking the system earns nothing

What they might tryWhy it doesn't work
Dawdle inside a housePay is on commission / allowed hours — taking longer earns the same money in more time, lowering their effective rate and turning their efficiency score red.
Pad idle / linger between jobsIdle isn't paid. Lingering earns $0.
Take the long way for more milesMileage is fixed point-to-point distance — route-independent.
Fake their locationGPS at check-in/out + a geofence flag catches off-site punches.
Linger into overtimeNo day clock to pad — the day auto-ends at the last check-out.

The captured idle / drive / efficiency data is the office's coaching radar — it tells you who to coach, not who to pay.
