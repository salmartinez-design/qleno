The Qleno Time Clock

One clock. Used at every house. That's the whole system.

We designed this together to be simple on purpose. There is no separate "check in" and "check out." There is no button to start or end your day. You tap Clock In when you arrive at a house and Clock Out when that house is done — that's it. This guide walks through every screen you'll see.

1. One clock pair, at each house

Each home on your day is its own clock. When you get to a house you tap Clock In. When you finish that house you tap Clock Out. Then you drive to the next house and do the same thing again.

1Arrive → tap Clock In. Your time and location are recorded.
2Cleaning → the clock runs. Tap Pause for a real meal break.
3Finished → tap Clock Out. The house shows Job Complete.

Why only one clock?

Two sets of buttons is what made the old app confusing. Your house clock-in/out is the only thing we need — it feeds your pay, your hours, and the drive between jobs. Nothing else to remember.

Pause Clock Out

An orange stripe means this house is active.

Once you're clocked in, the card gets an orange stripe and shows the time you started. You have two buttons:

PauseUse only for a real, sit-down meal break (30 min or more, fully off). It stops the clock so the break isn't counted as work. Tap Resume when you're back.
Clock OutTap when the house is finished and you're walking out. This ends the clock for this house.

Short breaks don't need a Pause

Grabbing water or a quick breather is just part of the job — leave the clock running. Pause is only for a full meal break.

3. Your day ends by itself

There is no "end my day" button. Your day is simply your first clock-in to your last clock-out. When you clock out of your last house, the app shows a Day Complete summary — jobs done and job hours.

If a surprise job gets added after that, no problem: you just clock in at the new house and your day stretches to fit it. You never have to "reopen" anything.

Why no day button?

A day button invites clocking out at home, which would pay for your drive home. By ending the day at your last house automatically, the math stays honest and simple — for you and for the office.

Basements and rural homes lose signal — your time is still kept.

Some houses have no cell signal — a deep basement, a home out in the country. Before, a clock-out in a dead zone could register late once you got back into signal. Not anymore.

If you tap Clock In or Clock Out with no signal, the app saves it right then with the real time and your location, and shows a yellow "waiting to sync" banner. The moment your phone is back online, it sends automatically — recorded at the time you actually tapped, not when the signal came back.

  • Keep working normally — just tap the buttons as usual.
  • The yellow banner clears itself once everything syncs.
  • Your real on-site time is what counts, every time.

You don't have to do anything. Tap the button, keep going. Qleno handles the rest as soon as you have signal.

5. How you get paid

Two things make up your pay. Everything else the clock records is just for the office to see the day clearly — it is not paid by the hour.

CommissionYour share of each job. This is the main thing you earn.
MileageThe drive between your houses, reimbursed per mile. The drive from home to your first job and from your last job home are not included.
Hours (for records)Job hours, drive time, and idle time are tracked so the office can coach and check the day. They are not a separate hourly paycheck.

Allowed hours vs. actual hours

Each job has an allowed number of hours (the budget) and your actual clocked hours. Beating the allowed time is good — it's the efficiency the Compensation training covers. The clock is what measures it, so always clock in and out at every house.

6. For the office

Owners and managers get extra visibility on the Time Clock screen — techs never see these:

  • GPS per punch — distance from the job and a flag if a punch was off-site.
  • Edit clocks — fix a missed or wrong punch directly from the job.
  • Mileage review — drive legs are calculated automatically but only become pay after you review and apply them. No money moves on its own.

The whole system in one line

Clock in at the house, clock out at the house, drive to the next one. The day, the hours, the mileage, and the pay all build themselves from those taps — even when you lose signal.
